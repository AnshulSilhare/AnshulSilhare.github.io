Operational Analytics & Revenue Optimization

Rapido Logistics: Operational & Revenue Optimization

RoleData Analyst
Dataset30,000+ Ride Bookings
DomainLast-Mile Logistics
Tech StackMySQL 8.0, Power BI

Situation

Rapido is a leading last-mile ride-hailing and logistics player. At high transaction volumes, tracking booking operational efficiencies vs. actual payment collections in flat sheets is error-prone. Fragmented records on driver cancellations, customer timeouts, and shift timings masked significant leaks in margins and bottlenecks in driver-supply availability.

Task

I was tasked with migrating flat transactional ride data into a centralized, robust database backend. The goals were to audit and segment the 30,000 bookings using SQL queries, calculate total revenue leakage, and map operational SLA failures to help planners optimize driver routing and dispatch schedules.

Action

To establish a query-optimized analytics environment, I implemented a Star Schema database model and analyzed performance metrics:

  • Database Normalization: Migrated base logs into a Star Schema structure of 8 tables (2 Fact tables: Fact_Bookings, Fact_Incomplete_Logs; and 6 Dimension tables: Dim_Customers, Dim_Drivers, Dim_Locations, Dim_Vehicle_Types, Dim_Fares, and Dim_Time_Schedules). Enforced 3NF normal form to eliminate data anomalies.
  • Revenue Auditing: Constructed subqueries to isolate actual ride payment amounts from baseline expected tariffs, flagging instances of leakage.
  • Turnaround Time (TAT) Analysis: Computed and compared operational duration columns (Pickup_Time - Booking_Time + Trip_Duration) grouped by shifts to identify service lag patterns.
  • Interactive Dashboarding: Connected the MySQL data warehouse to Power BI, mapping geo-locations of incomplete logs to guide field planners.

Result

Key Audit Insights
  • ₹251.9K Revenue Leakage Identified: Audited 30,000 bookings and uncovered ₹251.9K in uncaptured revenue, primarily caused by cancellation spikes and pricing model mismatches.
  • Shift Lead Time Bottlenecks: Analysis proved that Night Shifts (10 PM – 6 AM) experienced a 20% higher Turnaround Time (TAT) compared to day peak hours, indicating critical driver supply deficits at night.
  • Star Schema Architecture: Normalizing the transactional database reduced warehouse storage overhead and significantly improved query execution speeds for operational reporting.

Reflection

This project highlights that relational modeling is essential to identify leaks. Relying on flat-file spreadsheets often masks data discrepancies. Enforcing relational mapping across transaction details (booking, logs, and payments) is key to protecting margins in operations heavy last-mile logistics.
