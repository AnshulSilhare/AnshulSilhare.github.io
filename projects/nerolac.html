Internship Case Study

Nerolac Nerofix: Supply Chain Performance Analysis

RoleSupply Chain Analytics Intern
OrganizationKansai Nerolac Nerofix Pvt. Ltd.
TimelineMay 2026 – Present
Tech StackSQL, Power BI, Excel, Odoo ERP

Situation

Kansai Nerolac Nerofix is a leading manufacturer of industrial/automotive coatings and adhesives. The company faced operational gaps in supply chain visibility. Specifically, there was no centralized framework to audit transporter compliance against agreed Service Level Agreements (SLAs). This lack of visibility caused frequent depot stockouts, inflated inventory holding costs at local distribution points, and poor carrier selection decisions.

Task

I was tasked with auditing delivery data and designing a data-driven system to achieve two core objectives:

  • Establish a Transporter Benchmarking Framework by measuring transporter lead times and evaluating SLA compliance.
  • Perform statistical analysis of historical sales and stock levels to design scientific Safety Stock and Reorder Points (ROP) to optimize inventory levels and prevent stockouts.

Action

To execute this, I implemented a structured five-step workflow:

  • ERP Data Auditing: Extracted 6+ months of raw transit records from Odoo ERP. Validated timestamp logs by cleaning anomalous entry timestamps (e.g. gate logs entered retrospectively).
  • SLA Metric Architecture: Built SQL logic to compute actual transit times against standard transit matrices defined by plant-to-depot routes.
  • Interactive Dashboards: Engineered a Power BI Dashboard to track transporter metrics: compliance rate (%), average lead time deviation, volume handled, and transit variability (standard deviation).
  • Safety Stock Modeling: Applied statistical formulas in Excel/Python using standard formulas:
    Safety Stock = Z × σLT × Davg
    Where Z represents service factor (set to 95%), σLT is the lead time standard deviation, and Davg is the average daily demand.
  • Reorder Point Implementation: Computed ROP metrics based on the formula \(ROP = (D_{avg} \times LT_{avg}) + Safety\ Stock\) to define clear operational triggers.

Result

Quantifiable Outcomes (Operational Projections)
  • transporter Compliance Visibility: Mapped carrier compliance levels across 8 depots, identifying that carrier SLA compliance ranged widely between 55% and 92%.
  • Holding Cost Reduction: By adjusting Safety Stock levels based on statistical standard deviation instead of static rules-of-thumb, calculated a projected 12% reduction in local holding costs.
  • Stockout Prevention: Safety stock buffer adjustments mapped to protect key SKUs from stockouts during lead time spikes, projecting a 15% stockout rate reduction.

Reflection

This project highlighted that data quality is the biggest blocker to effective supply chain analytics. Incomplete or delayed gate entry logs skew actual lead time metrics. If I were to execute this project again, I would advocate for an automated barcode or RFID gate-logging integration within the ERP backend to eliminate manual timestamp errors at depots.
