Situation

In high-volume e-commerce supply chains, balancing SLA commitments against Capital Expenditure (CapEx) is a major optimization problem. A legacy 3-node distribution network struggled to meet the <48-hour shipping window, forcing reliance on premium transport carriers. To lower transport costs and keep shipping times fast, the company needed a consolidated hub-and-spoke configuration.

Task

My goal was to engineer a digital twin model of a 15-demand-node US network, evaluating spatial distances to select and stress-test an optimal 5-node regional distribution center (DC) strategy. The target was to cover 90% of the US population within a 48-hour transit window while minimizing total fuel consumption and shipping radius.

Action

I utilized a quantitative operations research approach to design, validate, and visualize the supply chain network:

Network Flow Simulation (US Hub-and-Spoke Routing)
  • Geospatial Geodesic Modeling: Programmed a Python scripts using Geopy to calculate geodesic distances (great-circle distance) between 15 main US city demand locations and candidate distribution sites.
  • Hub Selection Allocation: Modeled and allocated demand nodes to 5 optimized hub locations based on proximity, transport links, and volume capacity constraints:
    • Redlands, CA (ONT9) — Western Region
    • Robbinsville, NJ (EWR4) — Northeast Region
    • Louisville, KY (SDF2) — Midwest Region
    • Coppell, TX (DFW6) — Southern Region
    • Lakeland, FL (TPA2) — Southeast Region
  • Service Level Audit: Evaluated transit times across routes, validating that New York, Los Angeles, and Philadelphia could be serviced in 1 day from the nearest hub, while Houston, Boston, and Atlanta required 2 days, comfortably meeting the 48-hour SLA constraint.
  • Interactive Map Representation: Used Python/Folium to build a dynamic geospatial visualization showing active DCs, connected spokes, and delivery service zones.

Result

Reflection

This study demonstrates that network consolidation is a highly effective way to cut emissions and transport cost simultaneously. By reducing average delivery radius by 36%, we not only secured our 2-day delivery promise but also reduced carbon footprint. In future studies, I would add local variable tolling and carrier fuel-surcharges into the cost function for even greater pricing accuracy.
