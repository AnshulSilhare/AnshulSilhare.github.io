Statistical Forecasting & Inventory Planning

Pharma Demand Forecasting: Model Selection

DomainPharmaceutical Supply Chain
FocusModel Selection per SKU
Main BenefitExpiry Waste & Stockout Mitigation
Tech StackAdvanced Excel, Statistical Modeling

Situation

Pharmaceutical supply chains carry severe penalties for planning errors. Under-forecasting critical drugs leads to healthcare-damaging stockouts, while over-forecasting leads to high inventory write-offs due to product expiration. Standard static "one-size-fits-all" forecasting models fail because different drugs exhibit completely different demand patterns depending on prescribing rates and shelf-life constraints.

Task

My goal was to analyze the historical demand profiles of a multi-product pharmaceutical portfolio, segment them into distinct behavioral categories, and identify the optimal statistical forecasting model for each product class to minimize waste and ensure supply-line continuity.

Action

I implemented a portfolio segmentation and statistical evaluation framework:

  • Portfolio Segmentation: Analyzed demand coefficients of variation to categorize the SKUs:
    • Product A (High Volatility): Characterized by large, unpredictable daily orders and sudden drops, common in specialty medications.
    • Product B (Stable Demand): Consistent, high-volume demand with minor daily white-noise deviations, common in chronic care drugs.
  • Statistical Model Tuning: Evaluated and selected models for each segment:
    • For Product A (Volatile): Implemented Exponential Smoothing (Alpha = 0.5). Setting a high Alpha parameter allows the forecast to weight recent surges heavily, adapting quickly to sudden demand spikes rather than lagging behind a rolling average.
    • For Product B (Stable): Implemented a Moving Average model, which successfully smoothed out minor daily noise to prevent over-ordering.
  • Inventory Safety Bounds: Standard formulas were implemented to determine reorder thresholds based on the model forecast outputs and lead time variability.

Result

Forecasting & Operational Impact
Product Category Selected Engine Strategic Operational Logic
Volatile (Product A) Exponential Smoothing (α = 0.5) Allows the forecast to react immediately to sudden surges, preventing stockouts during critical healthcare windows.
Stable (Product B) Moving Average Smooths out minor daily noise, preventing high fluctuations in production scheduling and reducing inventory carry cost.

Reflection

This study proved that SKU segmentation is a prerequisite for inventory accuracy. Forcing a single forecasting technique across all SKUs inevitably over-buffers stable products and under-buffers volatile ones. Segmenting products based on volatility yields a much leaner supply chain and prevents expensive disposal of expired pharmaceutical stock.
